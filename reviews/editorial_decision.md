## Decision

### **Major Revision**

Subtype: *Revise and Resubmit with Structural Reframing Required*

The manuscript is not rejected. It contains a genuine empirical and theoretical contribution and is worth developing for publication. However, the revision requires structural changes to the confirmatory/exploratory hierarchy, removal or fundamental reframing of the ROC circularity claim, correction of the CL decomposition interpretation given α=.31, and recalibration of the practical implications to match the actual construct and sample scope. A re-review is required.

## Consensus Analysis

### Points of Agreement (Consensus)

**[CONSENSUS-5]** (All reviewers agree):

1. **Pre-registered RCT design is a genuine asset.** All five reviewers recognise the RCT design, ITT sensitivity analyses, and transparent power reporting as substantively stronger than most published GBL research. This is not in dispute.

2. **The mediation finding (SE fully mediates ENG→ADAPTS) is the paper's most robust positive finding.** All reviewers accept this as a meaningful within-game-group contribution. The Devil's Advocate explicitly designates it as a "non-defect" and the EIC describes it as "qualitatively different from simple outcome comparison."

3. **The germane CL subscale α=.31 undermines the CL decomposition as a mechanistic claim.** Reviewer 1 flags this as Critical; the EIC flags it; the Devil's Advocate flags it as Minor but consistent. The paper cannot present the extraneous/germane dissociation as empirically established when the germane subscale is dominated by measurement error.

4. **The confirmatory/exploratory hierarchy is inverted.** The pre-registered continuous moderation analysis is the correct primary evidence for expertise-conditional effects. The unregistered cluster analysis is exploratory. This ordering is inverted in the manuscript and must be corrected.

5. **Theoretical contributions (MTC, BPD) are conceptually compelling but lack operationalisation criteria.** All reviewers agree the concepts are intellectually interesting; the disagreement is about severity (EIC: Major; Reviewer 2: Minor; Devil's Advocate: Major; others: implicit).

**[CONSENSUS-4]** (4/5 reviewers agree):

6. **The ROC "deployable screening threshold" is overconfident.** EIC, Reviewer 1, Reviewer 3, and Devil's Advocate all flag the AUC=0.985 from n=10 positives as a precision claim the sample cannot support. (Reviewer 2 does not specifically address ROC.) The CRITICAL-1 finding from the Devil's Advocate — that the ROC analysis predicts cluster membership using the same variable used to define the clusters — is a circularity that EIC and Reviewer 1 independently note as a sample-size and precision concern.

7. **Practical recommendations need sample-scope qualification.** EIC, Reviewer 3, and the Devil's Advocate all note that the "B2B sales training" framing of the recommendations overstates the generalisability from a sample that is 69% education-sector, voluntary, Southeast Asian enrolment.

8. **d=−1.88 (C1 novice disadvantage) requires confidence intervals and explicit n reporting in the Results.** EIC, Reviewer 1, and Devil's Advocate agree the cluster-conditional effect sizes for C1 (derived from n=4 game-arm completers) are not accompanied by CIs and should be.

### Points of Disagreement

**Disagreement 1: Overall Recommendation Severity**
- **Reviewer 2 view**: Minor Revision — the paper is publication-ready with targeted additions to the game description and literature review.
- **EIC, R1, R3 view**: Major Revision — structural reframing of confirmatory/exploratory hierarchy and correction of CL reliability interpretation are substantive changes requiring re-review.
- **Disagreement type**: Severity disagreement
- **Editor's Resolution**: Major Revision. Reviewer 2's more positive assessment reflects the strength of the domain contribution and is well-founded from a GBL theory perspective. However, the structural problem identified by Reviewer 1 (inverted confirmatory/exploratory hierarchy with a Critical-severity ANCOVA assumption gap) and the Devil's Advocate's CRITICAL findings (ROC circularity, survivor bias unquantified) constitute revisions sufficiently substantive to warrant re-review. Reviewer 2's concerns are primarily additive (supplementary LM-GM table, literature gaps) and do not require re-review once addressed; the EIC, R1, and R3 concerns require structural rewriting.
- **Resolution Rationale**: The conservative principle applies where the revision touches the paper's core evidential claims and their presentation hierarchy.

**Disagreement 2: Severity of Format–Measurement Alignment Concern**
- **R3 and Devil's Advocate view**: This is more serious than a named Limitation — it threatens the validity of the primary outcome comparison and should be elevated in the Discussion.
- **EIC view**: Appropriately named in Limitations; supplementary analysis suggested but not required.
- **Reviewer 2 view**: Not specifically raised.
- **Disagreement type**: Severity disagreement
- **Editor's Resolution**: The paper should elevate the format–measurement alignment concern in the Discussion (not only in Limitations §6.8) and explicitly discuss the within-game mediation evidence as a partial counter-argument (if SE fully mediates ENG→ADAPTS inside the game, format matching alone cannot explain the game group's internal architecture). A supplementary analysis is encouraged but not required for the revision.
- **Resolution Rationale**: The internal mediation evidence does partial mitigation work that the paper does not currently articulate against this alternative explanation.

**Disagreement 3: Whether Gaming Experience as Alternative Moderator Constitutes a Critical Gap**
- **Devil's Advocate view**: The absence of a gaming experience measure is a serious gap because Hernández & Moreno (2019) characterise the moderator as gaming experience, not domain expertise.
- **Other reviewers**: Noted but not flagged as Critical.
- **Editor's Resolution**: The paper should add one explicit paragraph in §6.8 acknowledging that gaming experience was not measured and that the present study cannot distinguish domain expertise from gaming fluency as the operative moderator — especially for the C1 novice cluster. This does not require new data; it requires honest framing.
- **Resolution Rationale**: The expertise vs. gaming-fluency distinction is the paper's moderator interpretive claim, and the paper should be transparent about the measurement gap.

## Decision Rationale

This manuscript reports a pre-registered RCT of a game-based B2B sales training intervention, presenting both a null primary outcome and a rich set of exploratory findings that are theoretically coherent and consistent with the prior GBL literature. The reviewers broadly agree that the study is worth publishing; the disagreement is about how much reframing is required before it is ready.

The editorial decision of Major Revision is driven by three findings that, taken together, require structural revision rather than additive correction:

First, the reviewers unanimously identified an inverted confirmatory/exploratory hierarchy. The pre-registered continuous moderation (F(1,59)=11.65, p=.0012) is subordinated to the unregistered cluster analysis (C1 d=−1.88) in the manuscript's argumentative structure. This inversion misleads readers about what was pre-registered, and inflates the apparent precision of the cluster-derived findings. Correcting this requires restructuring §5 and revising the Discussion — not trivial editing.

Second, the CL decomposition interpretation is empirically untenable given α=.31 for the germane subscale. The "extraneous vs. germane" dissociation is invoked repeatedly in §6.2 and §7 as mechanistic explanation for the game's cognitive load profile. With CL1 dominated by measurement error, this interpretation cannot stand and must be revised to a descriptive trend framing throughout.

Third, the ROC "deployable screening criterion" is simultaneously circular (the classification feature partly defined the clusters being classified) and statistically overconfident (AUC=0.985 from n=10 positives). This requires removal or fundamental reframing — a change the authors can make in one or two paragraphs but that is conceptually significant.

On the positive side: the mediation finding, the Fisher z coupling comparison, the ITT analyses, and the LM-GM framework extension (once operationalisation is scoped appropriately as "conceptual proposals") are all genuine contributions that will survive the revision intact. Reviewer 2's more positive assessment correctly identifies these as publishable-quality contributions. The paper's honest Limitations section and transparent power reporting are exemplary.

### Required Item Details

**R1: Restructure Confirmatory/Exploratory Hierarchy**
- **Problem**: §5.6 (cluster analysis, unregistered) currently precedes §5.7 (continuous moderation, pre-registered), and the Discussion leads with cluster findings. This inverts the evidential hierarchy.
- **Source**: R1 (Critical), EIC (Critical), DA (Major)
- **Requirement**: Rename §5.7 to "§5.6: Primary Expertise-Effect Analysis (Pre-Registered Continuous Moderation)" and move it before the cluster analysis section, which becomes "§5.7: Exploratory Expertise Profiling (Unregistered Cluster Analysis)." Revise §6.4 and §7 to lead with continuous moderation as confirmatory and treat cluster d-values as exploratory illustration.
- **Acceptance criteria**: After revision, a reader who only reads the paper's section headers and topic sentences will understand which analyses were pre-registered and which were exploratory.

**R2: ROC Threshold Reframing**
- **Problem**: AUC=0.985 is presented as a "deployable screening criterion" (§6.7, Conclusion). The AUC is derived from predicting C1 cluster membership using pre-ADAPTS — the same variable used to define the clusters. This circularity produces an inflated AUC that does not estimate predictive validity for real-world novice identification.
- **Source**: EIC (Critical), DA (Critical-1), R1 (Major), R3 (implied)
- **Requirement**: Add a sentence in §5.7 noting that "the AUC should be interpreted cautiously: the classification feature (pre-ADAPTS) was also one of two features used to define the clusters, which partially inflates apparent discrimination." Change "deployable" to "provisional" throughout. Add bootstrap 95% CI on AUC. State explicitly that external validation in an independent sample is required before deployment.
- **Acceptance criteria**: The ROC finding is presented as a hypothesis-generating result with an appropriate caveat about circular derivation and small n.

**R3: CL Decomposition Reframing**
- **Problem**: The extraneous/germane load dissociation (CL2+CL3 d=0.90 vs. CL1 d=0.13 ns) is used as mechanistic evidence in §6.2 and the "CL as currency" metaphor in §7, but CL1 (directed effort / germane) has α=.31. The dissociation may reflect measurement noise in CL1, not a genuine load-type difference.
- **Source**: R1 (Critical), EIC, DA (Minor in isolation but compounding)
- **Requirement**: In §5.4 Results, add parenthetical "(germane subscale α=.31, interpreted as descriptive trend only)" after reporting CL1 d=0.13. In §6.2, change "the dissociation is theoretically coherent" to "the directional dissociation — if real — would be consistent with extraneous rather than germane load dominance; however, the germane subscale's poor internal consistency (α=.31) prevents definitive inference about load type." In §7, mark the extraneous/germane interpretation with the same caveat or reframe using only the CL2+CL3 elevation as the established finding.
- **Acceptance criteria**: No reader can come away from the paper believing the extraneous/germane dissociation is empirically established.

**R4: CI on Cluster-Conditional Effect Sizes**
- **Problem**: d=−1.88 (C1), d=−0.15 (C2), d=+0.24 (C3) are reported without confidence intervals. n=4 game-arm completers in C1 means the CI on d=−1.88 is extremely wide.
- **Requirement**: Add bootstrapped 95% CIs on all three cluster-conditional d values. Add a column to Table 5.5 showing n per condition (game vs. control) within each cluster.
- **Acceptance criteria**: d=−1.88 is accompanied by its CI and the n=4 game-arm denominator is visible in the table.

**R5: ANCOVA Assumption Tests**
- **Problem**: Homogeneity of regression slopes assumption may be violated for ADAPTS, given the significant pre-ADAPTS × condition interaction.
- **Requirement**: Report homogeneity of regression slopes test F and p for each primary outcome. If violated for ADAPTS, add a sentence in §5.1 noting that ANCOVA F for ADAPTS should be interpreted with caution and that the moderated regression analysis in §5.7 provides a more appropriate primary test of the ADAPTS effect under the heterogeneous slopes condition.
- **Acceptance criteria**: ANCOVA assumption testing is reported and its implications for the ADAPTS null are addressed.

**R6: ADAPTS Construct Qualification**
- **Problem**: "Adaptive selling" appears throughout §6.7 and §7 as if it means demonstrated adaptive selling performance rather than self-reported beliefs.
- **Requirement**: Replace "adaptive selling competency/skills/outcomes" with "self-reported adaptive selling beliefs" (or equivalent) consistently in §6.7 and §7. Add one sentence in the Conclusion calling for objective/behavioural outcome measures in future studies.
- **Acceptance criteria**: No conflation of belief measure with behavioural competency in the practical implications section.

**R7: LM-GM Alignment Supplementary Table**
- **Problem**: The LM-GM alignment is asserted but not documented, making the theoretical contribution unverifiable.
- **Requirement**: Supplementary Table A1 mapping each game mechanic (token economy, card selection, cascading information, Pavlovian feedback, Phase 2 role-play scenarios) to: intended learning mechanic per Arnab et al. (2015) taxonomy, Bloom level, and targeted sales competency.
- **Acceptance criteria**: A reader can verify whether each mechanic genuinely aligns with its claimed learning function.

**R8: Gaming Experience as Alternative Moderator**
- **Problem**: Gaming experience was not measured; the paper's domain expertise moderator interpretation cannot rule out gaming fluency as the operative mechanism.
- **Requirement**: Add one paragraph in §6.8 stating: "A further limitation is the absence of a gaming experience measure. The expertise reversal literature (Hernández & Moreno, 2019) characterises the moderator as gaming experience rather than domain expertise. The present study operationalised expertise as pre-training adaptive selling knowledge (ADAPTS-SV and SE scores), but these measures cannot distinguish low domain schema from low game fluency in the novice cluster. Future studies should measure gaming experience independently to disentangle these moderator interpretations."
- **Acceptance criteria**: The moderator interpretation uncertainty is explicitly acknowledged in §6.8.
