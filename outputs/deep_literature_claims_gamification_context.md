# Deep Literature Integration: Gameful Design, Mechanism, and Context

This memo records the deeper use of Nacke and Deterding, Mekler, and Landers/Armstrong for strengthening the thesis argument. The point is not to add more citations. The point is to sharpen the claim that this study answers a mature question in game-based learning: how, when, and for whom does a game-based design work?

## 1. Nacke and Deterding (2017): Mature research asks how, when, and for whom

Nacke and Deterding position gamification research as moving beyond first-wave questions such as "does gamification work?" toward differentiated questions about mechanisms, moderators, and application contexts. This is useful because the thesis should not frame itself as another binary game-versus-control comparison. It is stronger as a study of conditional effectiveness.

Use in thesis:

- Establish that the field has matured from broad effectiveness claims to mechanism-sensitive and context-sensitive evaluation.
- Justify the structure of the thesis: RQ1 tests the aggregate comparison; RQ2 explains why the aggregate comparison is insufficient.
- Strengthen the contribution: the thesis contributes a readiness-conditioned account of a game-based sales intervention, not simply a null result.

Best wording direction:

The study asks whether a pedagogically aligned game works, but more importantly it asks what the game demanded from learners and which learners were prepared to absorb that demand.

## 2. Mekler et al. (2017): Behavior can improve without the expected motivational pathway

Mekler et al. found that points, levels, and leaderboards increased output quantity in an image-annotation task, especially for levels and leaderboards, but did not improve output quality, perceived competence, autonomy, or intrinsic motivation. Their hypothesized competence-mediated motivation pathway was not supported. This matters because it breaks the easy chain: game elements -> motivation -> learning.

Use in thesis:

- Engagement should not be treated as the mechanism by itself.
- The mediation result becomes more valuable: the thesis tests whether engagement actually travels through self-efficacy into adaptive selling, instead of assuming that engagement is enough.
- The telemetry argument becomes more nuanced: logs can show behavior and output volume, but psychometrics and quality/outcome measures are needed to interpret whether behavior reflects competence, motivation, friction, persistence, or low-value activity.

Best wording direction:

The game may create visible behavior without producing the psychological mechanism required for learning. Therefore, self-efficacy is not just another outcome; it is the test of whether engagement became psychologically productive.

## 3. Landers and Armstrong (2017): Gameful training is readiness-sensitive

Landers and Armstrong applied the Technology-Enhanced Training Effectiveness Model to gamification. They found that expected value for gamified instruction was moderated by game attitudes and video-game experience. Participants with positive game attitudes and greater experience expected more value from gamified instruction, while those with lower attitudes and experience expected more value from traditional PowerPoint-style instruction.

This is not identical to the present study's sales-expertise moderator, so the manuscript should not overclaim. Their outcome was anticipated training valence, not demonstrated learning. The useful move is theoretical: gameful instruction is not uniformly received, and prior readiness can determine whether the same design is perceived as valuable or costly.

The stronger connection to this thesis is their use of cognitive load theory. They argue that when learners have limited experience with a training technology, cognitive effort is diverted into learning the technology rather than learning the content. This is very close to the thesis's mechanics acquisition cost argument.

Use in thesis:

- Support the "for whom" framing.
- Provide a predecessor for the idea that game-based training should assess learner readiness before deployment.
- Strengthen the Cognitive Budget Framework: prior sales schema is a domain-specific readiness variable, just as game experience and game attitudes are format-specific readiness variables.

Best wording direction:

The present study extends readiness logic from game attitude and game experience to domain schema and sales self-belief.

## 4. Mechanism bridge for H2

Use in thesis:

- Defend H2 as not merely exploratory correlation hunting.
- Explain why self-efficacy mediation matters for design: it identifies whether the game's engagement pathway connected to an applied sales outcome.
- Connect LM--GM to motivational mechanisms. LM--GM aligns mechanics to learning functions; Mekler shows why the motivational mechanism cannot be assumed; Landers and Armstrong show why learner readiness shapes whether a technology-enhanced design is received as valuable.

Best wording direction:

LM--GM explains design alignment, while the gameful-learning evidence explains why alignment must be tested through mediators and moderators.

## 5. Improved thesis claims

### Claim A: The thesis is not a failed game study

The strongest reading is that the game was aligned but cognitively expensive. The disconfirmation is useful because it identifies a missing term in design frameworks: mechanics acquisition cost.

### Claim B: The field-level contribution is conditional effectiveness

The study contributes to the move from "do games work?" to "for whom, through what pathway, and under what conditions?"

### Claim C: Engagement is demoted from explanation to variable

The thesis should avoid saying that games work because they are engaging. Instead, engagement becomes meaningful only if it is coupled to self-efficacy, transfer intention, or adaptive selling.

### Claim D: Readiness has two faces

Gameful-learning literature often treats readiness as attitude toward games or prior game experience. This thesis treats readiness as domain schema and adaptive-selling belief. That is a useful extension because sales training is not primarily about whether learners like games, but whether they have enough prior sales schema to use the game productively.

### Claim E: Telemetry corroborates but does not replace psychometrics

Behavioral logs can show dropout, latency, phase completion, and in-game engagement. They cannot determine whether observed behavior reflects learning, motivation, confusion, or persistence without the psychological measures.

## 6. Reviewer-safe cautions

- Do not collapse gamification and DGBL. Use gamification as adjacent theory on game elements and mechanisms.
- Do not claim Landers and Armstrong proves expertise moderation. It supports readiness-sensitive reception of gameful training.
- Do not claim Mekler proves self-efficacy mediation. It justifies why the mediator should be tested rather than assumed.
- Do not overstate the Cognitive Budget Framework as validated. The current study motivates and illustrates it as a worked example.
